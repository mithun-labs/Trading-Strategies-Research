### 1. CTA Trend Bayesian Reanalysis (Benhamou et al., arXiv 2507.15876, Jul 2025)

- **Score:** 40 (Experimental) | latent 40 (capped to 40 — evidence auditability 4/10:
  arXiv preprint only, primary PDF inaccessible, no peer review, no open code; key parameters
  NOT REPORTED; METHODOLOGY PAPER: no new tradeable strategy; horizon-blending finding is
  conventional CTA wisdom; performance metrics of replication strategy NOT REPORTED)
- **Asset class:** CTA / Diversified Futures (SG Trend Index)
- **Timeframe:** Multi-horizon (short-term + long-term trend signals)
- **Core concept:** Bayesian graphical / Kalman state-space model dynamically decomposes
  SG Trend Index returns into three factors: short-term trend, long-term trend, and market
  beta. The framework estimates time-varying loadings and uses them to build linear factor
  mimickers. Key finding: multi-horizon blending outperforms single-horizon trend; short-term
  signals specifically improve drawdown protection by capturing early reversals. Replication
  correlation ~0.80 to SG Trend over a 5-year live period (Jun 2020–Jun 2025) CLAIMED.
- **Evidence status:** CLAIMED, UNVERIFIED — arXiv preprint (arXiv 2507.15876 + SSRN 5365038);
  primary PDF inaccessible; all metrics from secondary web search summaries only
- **Source links:**
  - arXiv 2507.15876: https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.15876
  - SSRN 5365038: https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=5365038
- **Verification level:** arXiv preprint + SSRN; not peer-reviewed; no independent replication
- **Analyst note:** This is a methodology paper, not a new strategy. The practical conclusion
  ("blend short and long-term trend signals") is conventional wisdom for CTA practitioners.
  The Bayesian quantification adds analytical rigor but no novel edge. The live window (June
  2020 – June 2025) includes 2022 — one of the best CTA years since the GFC — which may
  flatter the replication model. Not an implementation candidate.
