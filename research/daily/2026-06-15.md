### 1. Enhanced Momentum with Momentum Transformers (Mason et al., arXiv 2412.12516, Dec 2024)

- **Score:** 22 (Low Confidence) | latent 22 (capped to 22 — evidence auditability 2/10:
  arXiv preprint, data leakage detected/corrected, computational scope reductions, no open
  code, no peer review, no independent replication, max drawdown and cost model NOT REPORTED)
- **Asset class:** US Equities (individual stocks; specific index/universe NOT REPORTED)
- **Timeframe:** Daily
- **Core concept:** Extends the Oxford Momentum Transformer (Wood/Roberts/Zohren 2021,
  arXiv 2112.08534) — an attention-LSTM hybrid that outperformed benchmark TSMOM on
  100 liquid futures — to US individual equities. The architecture uses multi-head attention
  (direct access to all lookback steps) combined with LSTM (sequential local pattern capture)
  to produce daily continuous position weights. Results reported: Sharpe 1.12, average annual
  return 4.14% (post-leakage correction, test set of unknown duration, costs claimed but model
  opaque).
- **Evidence status:** CLAIMED, UNVERIFIED — arXiv preprint; data leakage found and corrected
  during development; primary PDF inaccessible (HTTP 403); results from abstract + web summaries
- **Source links:**
  - arXiv 2412.12516: https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.12516 (retrieved 2026-06-15)
  - Original Momentum Transformer (arXiv 2112.08534): https://arxiv.org/abs/2112.08534
  - Original code: https://github.com/kieranjwood/trading-momentum-transformer
- **Verification level:** arXiv preprint only; not peer-reviewed; no independent replication;
  primary PDF blocked
- **Analyst note:** The underlying architecture (Wood et al.) is credible Oxford research.
  This 7-page extension is a student-project-grade paper. The data leakage discovery is the
  most important finding: it demonstrates that the authors were not initially aware of a
  fundamental data hygiene issue (timestamp handling for multi-instance stocks). After
  correction, the Sharpe 1.12 is plausible but unverifiable. The omission of maximum
  drawdown, trade count, volatility, and cost model detail makes this unresearchable further
  without accessing the full paper. Not an implementation candidate.
