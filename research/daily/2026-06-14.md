## Strategies This Pass (ranked by confidence)

### 1. Characteristic-Managed Momentum — CMM (Beckmeyer & Wiedemann, JBF 2025)

- **Score:** 54 (Experimental) | latent 54 (capped to 54 — evidence auditability 6/10; JBF
  peer-reviewed CC BY 4.0 but no open code; bid-ask-only cost model insufficient for
  high-turnover long-short equity; OOS methodology not confirmed rolling; PEAD decay risk;
  no post-2022 data)
- **Asset class:** US Equities (cross-sectional long-short)
- **Timeframe:** Daily return weighting; monthly rebalancing (implied)
- **Core concept:** Machine learning assigns sparse, non-uniform weights to the ~231 daily returns
  in the momentum formation window. Just 2 of 231 days receive 30% of total weight on average;
  30 days receive >50%. The most informative days are earnings announcement reaction days,
  market-wide jump days, and days with large individual stock returns. The resulting CMM signal
  outperforms standard 12-1 momentum with: gross Sharpe 1.47 (vs 0.49), net Sharpe 0.77 (vs
  NOT REPORTED for standard), max drawdown −27.6% (vs −80.3% in 2009), and annualized return
  18.5% (vs 13.2%) with volatility 12.6% (vs 26.9%).
- **Evidence status:** CLAIMED, UNVERIFIED — published in Journal of Banking & Finance Vol. 181
  (2025), CC BY 4.0 open access; peer-reviewed methodology; full paper blocked at primary
  sources (HTTP 403); all metrics from web search summaries of the JBF publication
- **Source links:**
  - SSRN 5702162: https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=5702162
  - JBF DOI: 10.1016/j.jbankfin.2025.107565
- **Verification level:** Peer-reviewed (JBF); CC BY 4.0 open access in principle; no open code
- **Analyst note:** The mechanism (EA-day reactions are more informative about future momentum)
  is independently supported by the extensive PEAD literature. The dramatic drawdown improvement
  (-27.6% vs -80.3%) is the strategy's strongest selling point — momentum crashes are the #1
  practical objection to pure momentum. However, the cost model is insufficient: a net Sharpe
  reduction of 0.70 from bid-ask alone implies very high turnover, and commissions/market impact
  would further degrade performance. The PEAD decay post-2016 is the core mechanism risk. Score
  54 reflects a JBF-published paper with concrete performance data tempered by insufficient cost
  modeling, no open code, and an uncertain OOS methodology.

## Emerging Patterns

- **PEAD decay as systemic risk for EA-based strategies:** This run's CMM strategy and the
  discovered (but not researched) Christensen et al. paper both highlight the same trend: post-
  earnings announcement predictability has been declining since ~2016. Strategies built on EA-day
  signal concentration (CMM) or post-announcement drift (PEAD strategies broadly) may be
  structurally weakening. The CMM OOS period ending in 2022 is particularly concerning — it
  avoids the most efficiency-driven period of 2022-2026.
- **Discovery bottleneck:** 13 of the 14 URLs attempted across the discovery and research phases
  returned HTTP 403. The research archive is increasingly constrained by web access restrictions.
  Strategy information is recovered entirely from search engine snippets and secondary citations,
  creating an irreducible information gap on key methodological details (OOS methodology, cost
  modeling completeness, sub-period performance).
- **"Enhanced momentum" papers as a crowded subfield:** CMM, fractional-momentum-smoothing,
  factor-timing-momentum-regime-tai, network-momentum-trend-following, and enhanced-momentum-
  transformers (queue) all belong to the same cluster of "standard momentum is too noisy, here
  is a better weighting" papers. Multiple testing across this subfield is significant: at least
  10 papers in the literature claim to "improve momentum" by modifying the formation-period
  weighting or signal combination. The one that appears best in an OOS period 1983-2022 may simply
  be the best-drawn sample from the noise distribution.

## Red Flags Detected

1. **CMM bid-ask cost model insufficient:** Sharpe drops 0.70 from bid-ask alone on a
   monthly-rebalanced strategy. This magnitude of cost suggests either very high turnover (daily
   rebalancing elements?) or concentration in illiquid small-cap stocks where bid-ask is large.
   Either explanation implies real-world costs are much higher than reported.

2. **PEAD decay documented post-2016:** The Christensen et al. paper (arXiv 2601.08962)
   specifically tests post-announcement trading strategies and finds they are consistent with
   efficient price formation after 2016 — exactly the mechanism CMM relies on.

3. **Extreme weight sparsity without OOS confirmation of rolling re-estimation:** 2 days
   capturing 30% of weight from a 231-day window implies the ML found very specific dates. If
   the rolling OOS is not properly implemented, this is in-sample overfitting with OOS labeling.
