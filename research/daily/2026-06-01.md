## Emerging Patterns

1. **FinBERT temporal leakage is a structural risk in all NLP-for-finance backtests:** The Zhang
   (2025) paper is one of many that claims "we avoided look-ahead bias" while only addressing
   feature-level look-ahead (not using tomorrow's news today). The deeper, harder-to-detect issue
   is model-level temporal leakage: if the NLP model (FinBERT, GPT, etc.) was trained on text
   that spans the OOS period, the model's internal representations encode implicit knowledge of
   that period's market events. This is unfixable without retraining the NLP model with a
   time-advancing training cutoff — computationally prohibitive in most academic settings. Any
   future NLP-based strategy in the queue should be automatically flagged for this issue.

2. **Extreme Sharpe claims should be presumptively attributed to leakage:** Two strategies in the
   database now report Sharpe >3 — `forecast-to-fill-gold-futures` (Sharpe 2.88, score 37) and
   `fx-news-sentiment-xgboost` (Sharpe 5.87, score 30). Both scored Low Confidence. The pattern
   is consistent: extreme Sharpe in single-author arXiv preprints without peer review almost
   always reflects some form of leakage, overfitting, or look-ahead. The research database has
   now accumulated enough evidence to treat Sharpe >3 on an arXiv preprint as a near-definitive
   red flag rather than an extraordinary claim requiring extraordinary evidence.
