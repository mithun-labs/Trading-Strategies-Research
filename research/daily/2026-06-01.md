- **Source links:**
  - [arXiv 2505.16136](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.16136)

  - [GitHub: jandrews65/zhang2025 (reproduction framework)](https://github.com/jandrews65/zhang2025)
- **Verification level:** arXiv preprint; no institutional affiliation confirmed; code available
  but reproduction unconfirmed
- **Analyst note:** Both automatic scrutiny triggers fired. The Sharpe values (5.87, 4.65, 4.65)

  without any genuine forward-looking edge. Score: 30 (Low Confidence). NOT an implementation
  candidate.

## Emerging Patterns

1. **FinBERT temporal leakage is a structural risk in all NLP-for-finance backtests:** The Zhang
   (2025) paper is one of many that claims "we avoided look-ahead bias" while only addressing
   feature-level look-ahead (not using tomorrow's news today). The deeper, harder-to-detect issue
   is model-level temporal leakage: if the NLP model (FinBERT, GPT, etc.) was trained on text
   that spans the OOS period, the model's internal representations encode implicit knowledge of
   that period's market events. This is unfixable without retraining the NLP model with a
   time-advancing training cutoff — computationally prohibitive in most academic settings. Any
   future NLP-based strategy in the queue should be automatically flagged for this issue.

2. **Extreme Sharpe claims should be presumptively attributed to leakage:** Three strategies in
   the database now report Sharpe >3 — `forecast-to-fill-gold-futures` (Sharpe 2.88, score 37),
   `fx-news-sentiment-xgboost` (Sharpe 5.87, score 30), and `drift-regime-factor-singha`
   (Sharpe 13, score 21). All scored Low Confidence; none have peer review; all are
   single-author preprints. The pattern is consistent and the database has now accumulated
   enough evidence to treat Sharpe >3 on an arXiv preprint as a near-definitive red flag. The
   13-Sharpe paper represents the most extreme case yet — each escalation reinforces the
   inverse relationship between headline Sharpe and actual evidence quality.

3. **Volatility risk premium is the most documentable FX/vol factor still active:** `vix-etn-dual-approach`
   joins `vix-cmf-ml-walkforward` (score 45) and `hmm-rl-regime-taa` (score 44) as strategies
   exploiting VIX/volatility dynamics. Of these, the Zarattini/Aziz/Mele paper presents the most
   credible economic rationale. The VRP has survived post-2018 better than naïve short-vol strategies
   because the dual filter (VRP + term structure) avoids the tail-risk exposure that destroyed XIV.

4. **Short-vol strategy tail risk requires explicit drawdown reporting:** Any short-volatility
   strategy must report the maximum drawdown during Volmageddon (Feb 2018) and COVID (Mar 2020)
   for the analysis to be complete. Both are extreme regimes where daily realized-vol filters
   are structurally insufficient. Going forward, strategies in this class should be automatically
   flagged if max drawdown is NOT REPORTED.

5. **Regime-conditional factor activation is a high-risk data mining pattern:** `drift-regime-factor-singha`
   introduces a new failure mode: combining a regime gate with a cross-sectional signal, where
   the regime gate itself was discovered by optimization. This pattern (filter → apply signal
   only during filter-active periods → report OOS on the same dataset) produces artificially
   inflated Sharpe because the OOS period is not independent of the regime-discovery process.
   Randomization tests that only perturb the filter threshold (not the filter concept) provide
   weak protection. Future strategies that use regime filters should be scrutinized for whether
   the regime concept was specified a priori or discovered by parameter search.

6. **Cross-domain researchers in finance: higher multiple-testing risk.** The 13-Sharpe paper
   is authored by a physicist/astrophysicist (NASA GSFC / IIT Bombay). Three of the five
   lowest-confidence strategies this run were written by researchers with no institutional
   finance affiliation. Cross-domain authors bring legitimate statistical skills but often lack
   awareness of the specific multiple-testing pathologies documented in empirical finance
   (Harvey, Liu, Zhu 2016: most published factors don't survive t-stat correction). Author
   institutional affiliation in finance should be treated as a weak positive signal going forward.

### 4. Drift Regime Factor — 13-Sharpe OOS Cross-Sectional Equity Factor
- **Score:** 21 — LOW CONFIDENCE
- **Asset class:** US Equities (S&P 500 long-short cross-sectional)
- **Timeframe:** Daily
- **Core concept:** Per-stock "drift regime" filter (>60% positive trading days in trailing
  63-day window) gates a combined value + short-term reversal signal. When a stock is in a
  drift regime, the strategy takes long/short cross-sectional positions based on value+reversal
  ranking. When not in a drift regime, the position is flat. Claims Sharpe 13 OOS over
  2004–2024 (S&P 500), CAGR 158.6%, vol 12.0%, max DD -11.9%.
- **Evidence status:** CLAIMED, UNVERIFIED — arXiv preprint only (2511.12490, also SSRN 5754502,
  Singha, Nov 2025); single author with astrophysics background (NASA GSFC / IIT Bombay);
  no peer review; no independent replication; no institutional finance affiliation
- **Source links:**
  - [arXiv 2511.12490](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.12490)
  - [SSRN 5754502](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=5754502)
- **Verification level:** arXiv preprint + SSRN cross-post; no peer review; no institutional
  finance affiliation; no independent scrutiny found
- **Analyst note:** Both automatic scrutiny triggers fired. Sharpe 13 is 43-86× typical equity
  factor OOS Sharpe; CAGR 158.6% implies compounding from $1,000 to ~$180 billion in 20 years
  with only one drawdown exceeding 12%. This combination is mechanically implausible for any
  real long-short strategy. Most likely explanation: **regime selection is data mining** — the
  60%/63-day threshold was found by optimization, not by prior theory, and the paper's
  "1,000 randomization trials" corrects only for threshold perturbation, not for the full
  concept-level search space. Additionally: no transaction costs on a daily-rebalanced
  long-short equity strategy (presumptive invalidation); S&P 500 survivorship bias unaddressed;
  author lacks finance domain expertise for understanding factor-mining false discovery rates.
  Score: 21 (Low Confidence). NOT an implementation candidate.
