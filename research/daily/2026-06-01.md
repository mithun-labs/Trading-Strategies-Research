- **Core concept:** GDELT Project global news feed → FinBERT sentiment scoring → daily sentiment

  directional return for three macro instruments. 5-fold expanding-window cross-validation over
  2017–April 2025. Claimed Sharpe 5.87 (EUR/USD), 4.65 (USD/JPY), 4.65 (ZN). CAGR >50% FX.
- **Evidence status:** CLAIMED, UNVERIFIED — arXiv preprint only (2505.16136, Zhang, May 2025);
  no peer review; single author; reproduction framework exists but results pending
- **Source links:**
  - [arXiv 2505.16136](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.16136)
  - [GitHub: yukepenn/macro-news-sentiment-trading](https://github.com/yukepenn/macro-news-sentiment-trading)
  - [GitHub: jandrews65/zhang2025 (reproduction framework)](https://github.com/jandrews65/zhang2025)
- **Verification level:** arXiv preprint; no institutional affiliation confirmed; code available
  but reproduction unconfirmed
- **Analyst note:** Both automatic scrutiny triggers fired. The Sharpe values (5.87, 4.65, 4.65)
  are 10–15× typical CTA per-instrument Sharpe. The most likely explanation is **FinBERT temporal
  leakage**: the FinBERT model used to score 2017–2025 news was trained on financial text that
  includes events from within that same period, creating implicit forward-looking contamination
  that the paper's feature-level look-ahead bias check does not address. Secondary concern: the
  5-fold expanding-window CV setup means XGBoost hyperparameters are optimized on the same period
  reported as "OOS." Together these two issues fully account for the extreme reported metrics
  without any genuine forward-looking edge. Score: 30 (Low Confidence). NOT an implementation
  candidate.

## Emerging Patterns

1. **FinBERT temporal leakage is a structural risk in all NLP-for-finance backtests:** The Zhang
   (2025) paper is one of many that claims "we avoided look-ahead bias" while only addressing
   feature-level look-ahead (not using tomorrow's news today). The deeper, harder-to-detect issue
   is model-level temporal leakage: if the NLP model (FinBERT, GPT, etc.) was trained on text
   that spans the OOS period, the model's internal representations encode implicit knowledge of
   that period's market events. This is unfixable without retraining the NLP model with a
   time-advancing training cutoff — computationally prohibitive in most academic settings. Any
   future NLP-based strategy in the queue should be automatically flagged for this issue.

2. **Extreme Sharpe claims should be presumptively attributed to leakage:** Two strategies in the
   database now report Sharpe >3 — `forecast-to-fill-gold-futures` (Sharpe 2.88, score 37) and
   `fx-news-sentiment-xgboost` (Sharpe 5.87, score 30). Both scored Low Confidence. The pattern
   is consistent: extreme Sharpe in single-author arXiv preprints without peer review almost
   always reflects some form of leakage, overfitting, or look-ahead. The research database has
   now accumulated enough evidence to treat Sharpe >3 on an arXiv preprint as a near-definitive
   red flag rather than an extraordinary claim requiring extraordinary evidence.

3. **Volatility risk premium is the most documentable FX/vol factor still active:** `vix-etn-dual-approach`
   joins `vix-cmf-ml-walkforward` (score 45) and `hmm-rl-regime-taa` (score 44) as strategies
   exploiting VIX/volatility dynamics. Of these, the Zarattini/Aziz/Mele paper presents the most
   credible economic rationale. The VRP has survived post-2018 better than naïve short-vol strategies
   because the dual filter (VRP + term structure) avoids the tail-risk exposure that destroyed XIV.

4. **Short-vol strategy tail risk requires explicit drawdown reporting:** Any short-volatility
   strategy must report the maximum drawdown during Volmageddon (Feb 2018) and COVID (Mar 2020)
   for the analysis to be complete. Both are extreme regimes where daily realized-vol filters
   are structurally insufficient. Going forward, strategies in this class should be automatically
   flagged if max drawdown is NOT REPORTED.
