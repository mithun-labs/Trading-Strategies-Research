## Strategies This Pass (ranked by confidence)

### 1. The Intramonth Momentum Cycle (PreTOM) — Nathan, Suominen, Tasa (SSRN 6426026, March 2026)

- **Score:** 61 (Worth Researching) | latent 61 (no cap; Evidence auditability 6/10:
  described methodology with strong T+1 causal ID; not peer-reviewed; Sharpe/drawdown NOT REPORTED)
- **Asset class:** US Equities (CRSP, value-weighted); 19 developed markets
- **Timeframe:** Daily execution; 6-trading-day monthly holding (PreTOM window)
- **Core concept:** Standard cross-sectional WML (Winner-Minus-Loser) momentum portfolio held
  ONLY during the 6 trading days before each month-end (T−9 through T−4 relative to last trading
  day). Mechanism: investors sell their loser stocks to meet month-end payment obligations
  ("dash-for-cash"), creating concentrated selling pressure on losers in this window. The strategy
  is entirely loser-driven — winners show no PreTOM differential.
- **Evidence status:** CLAIMED, UNVERIFIED — working paper, not peer-reviewed
- **Source links:**
  - SSRN 6426026: https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=6426026
  - Quantpedia 2026 award: https://quantpedia.com/quantpedia-awards-2026-winners-announcement/
- **Verification level:** SSRN working paper; Quantpedia 2026 2nd place; not peer-reviewed
- **Analyst note:** The T+1 settlement shift (SEC, May 2024) provides rare quasi-causal
  identification: the predicted 1-day shift in the window was confirmed in individual stocks,
  the momentum spread, and mutual fund returns. This substantially rules out pure data mining.
  However, Sharpe and drawdown are NOT REPORTED from accessible sources — critical gaps for
  implementation assessment. Post-publication crowding risk is a real concern: as of June 2026,
  the PreTOM window is widely known in the quant community.

   Qiqi Liang, Licheng Sun (SSRN 4622831; **Journal of Empirical Finance, Vol. 82, 2025** —
   peer-reviewed). Applies a MAX filter (highest recent daily return) to the standard weekly
   short-term reversal strategy. High-MAX stocks (lottery-like) with past 1-week losses earn
   1.66% the following week vs 0.65% for low-MAX stocks. Mechanism: lottery-seeking investor
   overreaction → larger reversals. Effective only when retail order imbalance is highest quintile.
   Robust to market microstructure biases. Transaction cost discussion: "survives robustness tests"

   **Priority: HIGH — peer-reviewed JEF paper; clear mechanism; add to queue for next run.**
