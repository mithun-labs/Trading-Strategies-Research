### 1. Dynamic Multi-Pair Trading with DRL (Lebiedź & Ślepaczuk, arXiv 2606.04574, Jun 2026)

- **Score:** 35 (Low Confidence) | latent 35 (capped to 35 — evidence auditability 2/10:
  arXiv preprint only; primary content HTTP 403; no open code; no peer review; all specific
  metrics NOT REPORTED; IS/OOS each 1 year only)
- **Asset class:** Cryptocurrency Perpetual Futures (Binance USD-M)
- **Timeframe:** 1-hour candles
- **Core concept:** Hierarchical framework combining cointegration + Hurst exponent filtering
  ("Filter-then-Rank") for pair selection with a PPO+LSTM deep RL agent ("Fixed Risk, Adaptive
  Mean") for execution timing. Safety layer ("deterministic shielding") constrains the RL agent
  within fixed risk boundaries. Monthly backtesting windows with trade-to-trade compounding.
  IS: 2024, OOS: 2025.
- **Evidence status:** CLAIMED, UNVERIFIED — all primary content HTTP 403; web search snippets
  only; "substantially outperforms heuristic baseline at 10% significance" (block bootstrap) —
  not independently verified.
- **Source links:**
  - arXiv 2606.04574: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.04574
- **Verification level:** arXiv preprint; not peer-reviewed; no independent replication; no
  open code
- **Analyst note:** The Hurst exponent filter (R/S analysis) is a genuine methodological
  contribution — it addresses a known weakness of cointegration-alone pair selection (spurious
  cointegration in trending assets). The "safe RL" deterministic shielding concept is well-founded.
  However: (1) IS+OOS are each only 1 year, far too short for reliable DRL training in volatile
  crypto; (2) the 10% significance threshold is weak; (3) crypto 2025 was a bull market regime;
  (4) perpetual funding rates may not be modeled; (5) all specific metrics are inaccessible.
  Primary research value: the Hurst + cointegration filtering concept. Future work: access full
  PDF; monitor for code release; revisit if paper is peer-reviewed or receives OOS extension.
