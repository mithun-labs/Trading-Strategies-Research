## Strategies This Pass (ranked by confidence)

### 1. 0DTE Conditional Trading Rules — SPXW
- **Score:** 66 (Worth Researching)
- **Asset class:** US Equity Index Options (SPXW / SPX)
- **Timeframe:** Intraday — fixed entry 10:00 ET, hold to 16:00 ET settlement
- **Core concept:** A logistic regression classifier trained on 10:00 ET implied-state features (IV level, term structure slope, skew proxies, lagged realized SPX moments, lagged PnL) selects whether to enter and in which direction across six SPXW 0DTE multi-leg strategy families. The unconditional VRP is too small to monetize after costs; the conditional approach achieves OOS net SR 0.82–0.93 for the best strategy families over April 2019 – January 2026.
- **Evidence status:** CLAIMED, UNVERIFIED — SSRN working paper (Vilkov, Frankfurt School; not peer-reviewed; last updated February 2026). Open Python replication package on GitHub with pre-built derived panels. Related paper is R&R at Review of Financial Studies.
- **Source links:**
  - GitHub: https://github.com/vilkovgr/0dte-strategies (open Python replication package)
  - SSRN: https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=4641356 (working paper, HTTP 403)
- **Verification level:** Working paper with open code. Evidence auditability = 6/10 (code open, readable; raw data proprietary; not peer-reviewed). Capped at 74; latent 66 stays uncapped by the cap.
- **Analyst note:** This is the first pure-options strategy in the archive and the first systematic 0DTE paper reviewed. The strategy is appropriately humble: the paper itself states the unconditional VRP is too small to monetize. The conditional version's net SR of 0.82 is attractive but depends on features being predictive in a sample dominated by low-vol regime (2017–2023). The put ratio spread's tail risk (effectively short a net put beyond breakeven) is the key implementation concern. Implementation is Python-feasible but requires paid options data.

## Emerging Patterns

1. **The VRP requires conditioning, not blind short-vol.** Three separate strategies in the archive now document the same pattern: unconditional VRP exposure (0DTE options, VIX ETNs, VIX CMF) fails after costs, but conditional timing improves performance. This is a coherent emerging theme.

2. **Gross-to-net cost degradation as a quality filter.** The Vilkov paper's explicit finding — iron butterfly/condor converts from positive gross SR to negative net SR — is one of the clearest demonstrations in the archive of why costs matter for short-dated, high-turnover strategies. This validates the archive's cost-modeling evaluation dimension.

3. **Options strategies as a new research frontier.** This is the first systematic options strategy in the archive (44 strategies total). Related unexplored territory: long-vol conditional strategies, skew trading, systematic dispersion, calendar spread strategies.

## Red Flags Detected

1. **Put ratio spread tail risk underweighted:** The best conditional performer in the Vilkov paper has structural left-tail exposure (net short put beyond breakeven). The OOS max drawdown of 3.2% of underlying is based on 2019–2026 data, which does not include a 1987- or 2008-type intraday crash. This is a material out-of-sample risk.

2. **Iron butterfly/condor cost inversion:** Gross SR 0.77 → Net SR negative. This is not a red flag against the strategy itself but a strong warning for practitioners who might implement gross-Sharpe strategies without cost analysis.
