### 1. FX Good Carry — Dynamic Quality-Filtered Carry Trade (Run 24)
**Slug:** `fx-good-carry-bekaert-panayotov`
**Score:** 59 · Experimental
**Asset class:** Forex G10
**Timeframe:** Monthly rebalancing
**Core concept:** Bekaert/Panayotov (JFQA 2020; NBER WP 25420) argue that carry crash risk is concentrated in specific "bad carry" pairs (e.g., AUD/JPY). Dynamically excluding pairs whose inclusion impairs the portfolio's rolling Sharpe produces a "good carry" portfolio with higher Sharpe and sometimes positive skewness while retaining the rate-differential premium.
**Evidence status:** Peer-reviewed (JFQA top-4 journal, ~5-year review). Specific metrics (Sharpe, max DD, CAGR, win rate) NOT REPORTED from accessible summaries — HTTP 403 on all primary PDFs. Evidence auditability: 6/10.

### 2. FX Carry Conditional — Dupuy (2021) Risk-Adjusted Managed Carry (Run 25)
**Slug:** `fx-carry-conditional-dupuy`
**Score:** 54 · Experimental
**Asset class:** Forex G10 + EM (34–39 currencies)
**Timeframe:** Monthly rebalancing
**Core concept:** Dupuy (JBF 2021, Vol. 129) conditions carry trade deployment on three macro regime signals: (1) aggregate forward discount positive, (2) global FX volatility low, (3) cross-currency return dispersion low. When any condition fails, the strategy exits to flat. Claims to flip benchmark carry skewness from −0.76 to +0.97 while raising Sharpe from 0.76 to 1.07 (combined, 34 currencies, 1999-2018). G10-only improvement is "small, insignificant" (0.45→0.53); requires EM signal to reach 0.64 for G10.
**Evidence status:** Peer-reviewed (JBF Scopus Q1). Full paper paywalled. Quantitative findings from web search snippets. Evidence auditability: 6/10.
**Source links:**
- ScienceDirect (HTTP 403): https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S037842662100131X
- IDEAS/REPEC (HTTP 403): https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/jbfina/v129y2021ics037842662100131x.html
**Verification level:** CLAIMED, UNVERIFIED
**Analyst note:** The three conditions are sensible regime gates with economic rationale. However: (1) conditions may be calibrated to known crash episodes, (2) G10-only improvement is insignificant, (3) costs not confirmed modeled, (4) max drawdown not reported, (5) no post-2018 OOS. This strategy is architecturally simpler than good/bad carry but relies on cross-asset (EM) signal for meaningful G10 improvement. ## Emerging Patterns

### Carry crash-risk reduction: two parallel academic programs
The FX carry literature has developed two distinct approaches to the same problem:
1. **Pair selection** (Bekaert/Panayotov JFQA 2020): exclude "bad carry" pairs ex ante by Sharpe contribution test
2. **Macro regime gating** (Dupuy JBF 2021): exit all carry during unfavorable regimes (high vol, negative carry, high dispersion)

Both claim positive skewness. Both are peer-reviewed. Both lack post-2018 OOS data. The approaches are complementary and could in principle be combined (select good carry pairs, then gate on macro regime). No combined paper found in accessible sources.

A third approach — hedging crash risk directly (Daniel/Hodrick/Lu CFR 2017) — uses options overlays and is already archived under `fx-good-carry-bekaert-panayotov` context. That approach is more expensive (options premium cost) but removes tail risk without relying on timing.

### G10 carry premium state (2025 context)
- Post-GFC G10 carry Sharpe collapsed to ~0.25 through 2019 (near-zero rates)
- 2022–2024 rate divergence (Fed vs. BoJ especially) reinvigorated carry differentials
- August 2024 JPY carry unwind was a significant event — a managed carry strategy with vol condition would have exited in July/August 2024
- Current (2025) G10 rate environment: USD higher rates vs. JPY/CHF still provides positive carry; but Fed cutting cycle may compress this

## Red Flags Detected

- **HTTP 403 systemic barrier:** All academic PDF sources returned HTTP 403 throughout both runs. This prevents verification of threshold values, cost modeling details, and full drawdown statistics. All carry metrics remain CLAIMED, UNVERIFIED.
- **Carry premium compression risk:** Both strategies perform best when rate differentials are large and stable. The post-2026 rate path (further Fed cuts) could compress G10 carry back toward zero, making both strategies near-flat by construction.
- **Skewness claims not independently verified:** Both papers claim positive skewness for their managed portfolios. This is mechanically expected given that both strategies exit during high-volatility / crash episodes. The question is whether the conditions are predictive (exit before crash) or reactive (conditions turn bad at same time as crash). Neither paper's OOS evidence resolves this.
