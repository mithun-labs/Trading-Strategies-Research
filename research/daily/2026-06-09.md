## Strategies This Pass

### 1. FX Good Carry — Dynamic Quality-Filtered Carry Trade
**Slug:** `fx-good-carry-bekaert-panayotov`
**Score:** 59 · Experimental
**Asset class:** Forex G10
**Timeframe:** Monthly rebalancing
**Core concept:** Bekaert/Panayotov (JFQA 2020; NBER WP 25420) argue that the negative skewness of standard carry trades is not intrinsic to the carry premium but is concentrated in specific "bad carry" currency pairs (typically AUD, NOK vs. JPY). By dynamically excluding pairs whose inclusion would impair the preceding-period Sharpe of the portfolio, the residual "good carry" portfolio achieves higher Sharpe and sometimes positive skewness, while still capturing the structural interest rate differential premium.
**Evidence status:** Peer-reviewed (JFQA top-4 journal, ~5-year review); specific metrics (Sharpe, max DD, win rate) NOT REPORTED from accessible summaries due to HTTP 403 barriers on all primary PDFs. Evidence auditability: 6/10.

The plain carry trade already achieves ~60–70% profitable months (academic literature consensus), and the Bekaert/Panayotov quality filter specifically targets the tail-risk events that destroy overall profitability. ## Emerging Patterns

### FX win rate / RR tension
FX strategies advertising "high win rate + good risk/reward" simultaneously are systematically unverifiable in retail channels and frequently rely on:
1. The natural positive win rate of carry (which comes with negative skewness — "picking up nickels in front of a steamroller")
2. Short-duration scalps where nominal win rate is high but expectancy is flat after spread
3. Session breakout strategies (e.g., London breakout in archive) with weak evidence
The "good carry" approach is the only peer-reviewed strategy found that structurally addresses both dimensions — improving win rate persistence and reducing tail losses — though at monthly frequency rather than intraday.

### Carry trade quality differentiation
Multiple papers in the carry literature are now disaggregating the carry premium:
- Bekaert/Panayotov (2020): good vs. bad carry pairs (Sharpe contribution filter)
- Dupuy (JBF 2021): conditional carry using forward discount + realized volatility signals
- Daniel/Hodrick/Lu (CFR 2017): carry portfolios that hedge crash risk
All converge on the same insight: plain carry's negative skewness is not fundamental but reducible by selection or hedging. The specific implementations differ and their post-2015 performance is incompletely documented.
