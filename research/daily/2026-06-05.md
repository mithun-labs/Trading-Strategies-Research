## Strategies This Pass (ranked by confidence)

### 1. Multi-Asset TAA via Asset-Specific Regime Forecasts — 55 (Experimental)

- **Asset class:** Multi-Asset — 12 risky assets (global equity, bonds, REIT, commodity indices)
- **Timeframe:** Monthly rebalancing (inferred)
- **Core concept:** Two-stage framework: (1) Statistical Jump Model (SJM) classifies each of
  12 assets independently into bull/bear regimes; (2) per-asset gradient-boosted decision tree
  (GBDT) forecasts each asset's next-period regime using asset-specific + cross-asset macro
  features. Regime-conditional return/risk forecasts fed into Markowitz MVO for optimal weights.
  The key innovation is per-asset (not portfolio-level) regime forecasting, which allows the
  optimizer to hold bull-regime assets while simultaneously reducing bear-regime assets.
- **Evidence status:** `CLAIMED, UNVERIFIED` for specific metrics (all paywalled). The methodology
  is published in a peer-reviewed journal (AOR 2025) and is therefore AUDITABLE in structure.
  Performance numbers (Sharpe, CAGR, max DD) not retrievable from accessible sources.
- **Source links:**
  - https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.09578 (arXiv preprint)
  - https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10479-024-06266-0 (AOR 2025 publication)
  - https://github.com/Yizhan-Oliver-Shu/jump-models (jumpmodels open-source library)
- **Verification level:** Medium. Paper is peer-reviewed (AOR 2025). SJM component is open-source.
  Full GBDT+MVO pipeline is not open. No independent community replication found.
- **Analyst note:** The paper is methodologically rigorous and part of a well-developed research
  program from Princeton ORFE. The per-asset regime forecasting extends the single-regime approach
  (which is already peer-reviewed and open-code in the JAM 2024 paper) in a principled direction.
  The main open question is whether the GBDT regime forecasting layer adds real out-of-sample value
  or merely overfits the in-sample regime labels — this is the critical empirical question that
  only the full paper (or an independent replication) can answer.

  regime-switching-jump-model line of work have been researched (JAM 2024 → AOR 2025 → JPM 2025).
  Combined, they represent one of the more rigorous regime-switching TAA frameworks in current
  academic literature. The key remaining research gate for all three: access full paywalled papers

- **Multi-asset diversification at monthly frequency appears robust:** Several strategies
  researched over recent runs (gold-cross-asset-regimes, fx-carry-value-momentum, tsmom-china,
  dynamic-factor-shu-mulvey, this paper) all operate at monthly rebalancing with diversified
  asset universes. This frequency is less cost-sensitive than daily/intraday strategies and
  appears to be where the more credible evidence is concentrated.
- **Per-asset vs. portfolio-level regime identification is an important methodological axis:**
  This run highlights that the SJM applied per-asset (AOR 2025) outperforms portfolio-level SJM
  (JAM 2024). This principle extends beyond this specific framework — it's a general insight for
  multi-asset portfolio construction research.
