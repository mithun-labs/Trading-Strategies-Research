## Strategies This Pass (ranked by confidence)

### 1. Regime-Optimal Trend Following (OPT) — Zakamulin 2026

- **Score:** 54 (Experimental)
- **Asset class:** US Equity Portfolios / Equity Indices (Ken French library; also applicable to
  broad equity index ETFs/futures: SPY, QQQ, NAS100, EFA)
- **Timeframe:** Monthly
- **Core concept:** Replaces the binary ±1 TSM rule with regime-probability-weighted position
  sizing derived from per-regime Sharpe ratio estimates using an expanding window. In the 2-regime
  (Bull/Bear) setting, the strategy achieves an average OOS Sharpe of 0.506 vs. 0.208 for standard
  TSM across 18 Ken French datasets (1998–2025). In the 4-regime extension (Bull/Correction/Bear/
  Rebound), OPT achieves 0.628 vs. 0.496 for the Dynamic Speed Momentum (DSM) of Goulding/Harvey/
  Mazzoleni (JFE 2023).
- **Evidence status:** CLAIMED, UNVERIFIED — SSRN working paper (Zakamulin, University of Agder,
  March 2026; not peer-reviewed). Extension of peer-reviewed Zakamulin/Giner Journal of Asset
  Management 2024. Datasets are from the Ken French public library (reproducible, survivorship-free).
  Three independent review sites (CXO Advisory, Alpha Architect, Allocate Smartly) have covered the
  paper. Full text HTTP 403 — metrics from web search excerpts only.
- **Source links:**
  - SSRN: https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=6376479 (working paper)
  - Foundation (peer-reviewed): https://link.springer.com/article/10.1057/s41260-024-00357-0 (JAM 2024)
- **Verification level:** SSRN preprint with independent reviews from 3 sites. Evidence auditability
  = 5/10 (publicly reproducible data, described methodology, not independently confirmed). Capped
  at 74; latent 54 does not reach the cap.
- **Analyst note:** The key insight is that the OPT rule is theoretically grounded — it is not an
  ad-hoc improvement but the result of solving a Sharpe-maximization problem under regime uncertainty.
  This makes it meaningfully different from the other regime-based strategies in the archive. However,
  the gross Sharpe figures and absence of cost/drawdown data make a definitive assessment impossible.
  The single largest practical concern: the OOS Sharpe figures are likely gross, while transaction
  costs for monthly equity index rotation are minimal (~5–10bps round-trip), so the strategy's
  absolute performance net of costs may still be attractive for index-level implementation.

## Emerging Patterns

1. **Regime-graded position sizing vs. binary trend following.** The Zakamulin OPT framework
   formalizes something already implicit in `industry-trend-century` and the SJM-based strategies:
   the strongest improvement comes from sizing proportionally to regime quality, not from switching
   a binary flag. This is now a coherent sub-theme across 4 archive entries.

2. **Multiple-testing in academic validation.** The 18 Ken French datasets are highly correlated
   US equity portfolios. This is a recurring pattern in the equity factor/trend literature: papers
   report averages across many related datasets, creating an appearance of statistical robustness
   that is partly illusory due to cross-sectional correlation. This appears in Zakamulin (18
   correlated datasets), Singha (cross-sectional factors within S&P 500), and Shu/Mulvey (6 US
   equity factors). The effective independent test count is much smaller than the nominal N.

3. **HTTP 403 access barriers dominate today's pass.** Nearly all primary sources — SSRN, arXiv
   HTML, Alpha Architect, CXO Advisory, Allocate Smartly — returned 403 errors in this session.
   Only search-result excerpts and a limited number of open web pages were accessible. This is
   increasingly a systemic constraint on the research workflow. The source archive documents all
   attempted URLs.

## Red Flags Detected

1. **Transaction costs NOT REPORTED:** The Zakamulin OPT paper does not appear to model transaction
   costs in accessible summaries. The Sharpe figures are labeled "gross" in at least one review.
   For monthly equity index ETFs this is minor; for factor portfolios with higher turnover it is
   a meaningful omission.

2. **Maximum drawdown NOT REPORTED:** For any trend-following strategy, drawdown is the primary
   risk metric. Without it, the strategy's tail risk profile is unknown. An OPT strategy could
   theoretically have lower drawdown than TSM (since it sizes down in low-quality regimes), or
   higher (if regime misclassification concentrates losses). Unknown.

3. **18 correlated datasets:** The "18 datasets" are Ken French portfolios — all US equity, highly
   correlated. The effective independent sample is much smaller. Average Sharpe across correlated
   datasets inflates the appearance of robustness.
