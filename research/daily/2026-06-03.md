### 1. ML-Based Bitcoin Trading Under Transaction Costs — 46 (Experimental)

- **Asset class:** Bitcoin (BTC-USDT)
- **Timeframe:** Hourly (H1)
- **Core concept:** XGBoost, LSTM, and iTransformer models forecast hourly BTC returns using
  technical indicators in a 27-fold walk-forward protocol. A cost-aware execution filter
  (trade only when |predicted return| > 10bps) prevents trading when the signal is too small
  to survive costs. Best result: long-only XGBoost → CAGR > 65%, Sharpe > 1 after costs.
- **Evidence status:** `CLAIMED, UNVERIFIED` — arXiv preprint; not peer-reviewed; no open code
- **Source links:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.00060 (Bysik/Ślepaczuk, arXiv 2606.00060)
- **Verification level:** Low-moderate. Walk-forward methodology is rigorous (27 folds);
  costs explicitly modeled; but paper is unreviewed; no open code; max drawdown missing.
- **Analyst note:** The paper's most durable contribution is the **cost-aware filter concept**,
  not the specific XGBoost implementation. Naive sign-based strategies failing after 10bps
  costs is an important and honest finding. The CAGR > 65% should be compared against
  BTC buy-and-hold (also very high over 2018–2026) before drawing conclusions about ML edge.
  The maximum drawdown omission is the single biggest analytical gap — a long-only BTC
  strategy over 2018–2026 likely experienced 50%+ drawdowns during 2018, 2022, etc.

## Second Strategy: EM vs US Momentum TAA — 49 (Experimental)

- **Asset class:** Global Equities (EEM — Emerging Markets; SPY — US)
- **Timeframe:** Monthly rebalancing
- **Core concept:** Compare 6/9/12-month ROC or SMA momentum signals for EEM vs SPY.
  Long the outperforming asset, short the underperforming asset (long-short variant); or
  long only the winner (long-only variant). Blend horizons for composite signal.
- **Evidence status:** `CLAIMED, UNVERIFIED` — Quantpedia blog; not peer-reviewed.
- **Source links:** https://quantpedia.com/systematic-tactical-allocation-in-emerging-markets-vs-u-s-a-momentum-based-approach/ (Vojtko/Dujava, Apr 2026)
- **Verification level:** Low — blog post, no underlying academic paper, no OOS test.
- **Analyst note:** The strategy is elegant and implementable with freely available data.
  The core problem: its "best" configurations are essentially "be long US equities" for
  most of 2010–2026. The Sharpe of 0.564 is credible but not meaningfully higher than
  SPY buy-and-hold (~0.55–0.60 over the same period), once the long-short is decomposed.
  The genuine value would come from the signal in the EM-outperformance sub-period
  (2000–2010) — a sub-period analysis that is not reported.

## Third Strategy: Commodity Intra-Market Correlation Momentum-Reversal — 45 (Experimental)

- **Asset class:** Commodities (DBA agriculture, DBB base metals, DBE energy, DBP precious metals)
- **Timeframe:** Monthly rebalancing
- **Core concept:** 20-day vs 200-day pairwise correlation among the 4 ETFs determines
  strategy mode. High relative correlation → momentum (long top 2, short bottom 2).
  Low relative correlation → reversal (long bottom 2, short top 2). Combined strategy
  "nearly doubles" standalone momentum or reversal returns.
- **Evidence status:** `CLAIMED, UNVERIFIED` — SSRN working paper; not peer-reviewed;
  all specific metrics NOT REPORTED in accessible sources.
- **Source links:** https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=4964417 (Vojtko/Pauchlyová, Sep 2024)
- **Verification level:** Low — SSRN preprint, no peer review, no specific numbers,
  no OOS test. The universe of only 4 ETFs is a fundamental weakness.
- **Analyst note:** The concept (using correlation regimes to switch between momentum
  and reversal) is theoretically interesting and generalizable. However, 4 ETFs is far
  too few to establish statistical significance for a momentum strategy. "Nearly doubles"
  a near-zero baseline has limited meaning. The strategy is more notable as a design
  concept (correlation as regime signal) than as a deployable strategy at this universe size.

## Emerging Patterns

  crypto research. The principle — trade only when the signal magnitude exceeds the transaction
  cost threshold — is broadly applicable. It appeared previously (implicitly) in the TSMOM
