## Strategies This Pass (ranked by confidence)

### 1. ML-Based Bitcoin Trading Under Transaction Costs — 46 (Experimental)

- **Asset class:** Bitcoin (BTC-USDT)
- **Timeframe:** Hourly (H1)
- **Core concept:** XGBoost, LSTM, and iTransformer models forecast hourly BTC returns using
  technical indicators in a 27-fold walk-forward protocol. A cost-aware execution filter
  (trade only when |predicted return| > 10bps) prevents trading when the signal is too small
  to survive costs. Best result: long-only XGBoost → CAGR > 65%, Sharpe > 1 after costs.
- **Evidence status:** `CLAIMED, UNVERIFIED` — arXiv preprint; not peer-reviewed; no open code
- **Source links:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.00060 (Bysik/Ślepaczuk, arXiv 2606.00060)
- **Verification level:** Low-moderate. Walk-forward methodology is rigorous (27 folds);
  costs explicitly modeled; but paper is unreviewed; no open code; max drawdown missing.
- **Analyst note:** The paper's most durable contribution is the **cost-aware filter concept**,
  not the specific XGBoost implementation. Naive sign-based strategies failing after 10bps
  costs is an important and honest finding. The CAGR > 65% should be compared against
  BTC buy-and-hold (also very high over 2018–2026) before drawing conclusions about ML edge.
  The maximum drawdown omission is the single biggest analytical gap — a long-only BTC
  strategy over 2018–2026 likely experienced 50%+ drawdowns during 2018, 2022, etc.
