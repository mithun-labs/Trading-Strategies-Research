### 1. Bitcoin Multi-Timeframe Trend Strategy — D1H1 STOP
- **Score:** 33 — LOW CONFIDENCE
- **Asset class:** Bitcoin (BTC/USD), Gemini Exchange
- **Timeframe:** H1 entry / D1 trend filter
- **Core concept:** Apply Alexander Elder's "Trade with the Tide" principle to Bitcoin: use daily MACD direction to filter hourly MACD crossover entries. Three progressive versions: MACD Pure (Sharpe 0.33), D1H1 Filter (Sharpe 0.80), D1H1 STOP (Sharpe 1.07 / Calmar 0.87). Long-only design throughout.
- **Evidence status:** CLAIMED, UNVERIFIED — SSRN 5748642 (Mesíček/Vojtko, Quantpedia, Nov 2025); not peer-reviewed; no open code; no independent replication; all sources HTTP 403
- **Source links:** [SSRN 5748642](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=5748642) · [Quantpedia blog](https://quantpedia.com/how-to-design-a-simple-multi-timeframe-trend-strategy-on-bitcoin/)
- **Verification level:** No independent replication; working paper only; HTTP 403 blocked
- **Analyst note:** The Sharpe improvement from 0.33 to 1.07 is meaningful in relative terms but the strategy is long-only during a structural BTC bull market (2018–2025). Most critically, transaction costs are NOT modeled — ~143 trades/year at typical Gemini fees implies 14–70% annual cost burden against an implied CAGR of ~10–11% (ANALYSIS from Calmar). Presumptive invalidation pending cost-inclusive test. Third Vojtko/Quantpedia Bitcoin paper in the database; correlated evidence base.

### 2. Crypto TS and XS Momentum — Realistic Costs
- **Score:** 55 — EXPERIMENTAL
- **Asset class:** Cryptocurrency (~471 Binance coins, market cap + volume filtered)
- **Timeframe:** Daily signal, 5-day holding period
- **Core concept:** Test time-series (TS) and cross-sectional (XS) momentum on a large crypto universe with explicit transaction costs (15 bps per trade). TS momentum (long market when 28-day return positive, flat otherwise) survives: Sharpe 1.51 vs market 0.84. XS momentum (long winners, short losers) does NOT survive costs — many strategies with statistically significant gross returns are insignificant net. Momentum concentrated in large winners; losers mean-revert. Data: Jan 2017–Aug 2023, Binance, ~471 coins.
- **Evidence status:** CLAIMED, UNVERIFIED — SSRN 4675565 (Han/Kang/Ryu, Dec 2023, Sungkyunkwan University); not peer-reviewed; no open code; no independent replication; sources HTTP 403
- **Source links:** [SSRN 4675565](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=4675565)
- **Verification level:** Working paper only; no journal publication confirmed; no replication
- **Analyst note:** The paper's main contribution is making costs explicit — this is methodologically honest and unusual for crypto strategy papers. TS Sharpe 1.51 after 15bps/trade is plausible but the strategy is effectively a bull market timer (long-only in a 2017-2023 crypto window with two massive bull cycles). The XS failure is a high-information finding: shows that the more interesting multi-coin alpha strategy doesn't survive realistic conditions. Key gap: no OOS test; best parameters (28-day/5-day) selected in-sample. Score: 55 (Experimental).

### 3. Gold Cross-Asset Regimes — Dual Momentum GLD+IEF
- **Score:** 51 — EXPERIMENTAL
- **Asset class:** Gold (GLD ETF / XAUUSD)
- **Timeframe:** Monthly rebalancing
- **Core concept:** Long GLD when BOTH 12-month total return of GLD AND IEF are positive; otherwise move to cash. Based on the macro insight that gold's best performance occurs when both gold and long-duration Treasuries are trending up — a regime characterized by falling real rates and monetary easing. Independently replicated by Allocate Smartly.
- **Evidence status:** CLAIMED, UNVERIFIED — Quantpedia blog (Dujava, Dec 2025); independently replicated by Allocate Smartly; no peer review; no dedicated SSRN paper
- **Source links:** [Quantpedia blog](https://quantpedia.com/cross-asset-price-based-regimes-for-gold/) · [Allocate Smartly](https://allocatesmartly.com/gold-cross-asset-momentum/)
- **Verification level:** Practitioner replication by Allocate Smartly; no academic validation
- **Analyst note:** The rule is extremely simple (2 conditions, monthly, liquid ETFs) and has 33+ years of backtest data. Key tension: CAGR 8.5% underperforms B&H gold (~10.5%), making this purely a drawdown-reduction tool rather than a return enhancer. Max DD -33.7% is still substantial. Pre-Bretton Woods data in the extended 1969 sample is problematic (gold was price-fixed pre-1973). No OOS period documented. Score: 51 (Experimental).

### 4. Mining Gold for Regimes — PCA/K-Means Regime-Conditional TAA
- **Score:** 46 — EXPERIMENTAL
- **Asset class:** Gold (GLD/XAUUSD)
- **Timeframe:** Monthly (inferred from regime persistence)
- **Core concept:** PCA + k-means clustering (k=3, 1,000 iterations) identifies three latent macro regimes driving gold returns: "stable currency," "real asset inflation hedge," and post-GFC low-rate regime (appeared Dec 2008). Tactical allocation based on identified regime outperforms benchmarks. Regime persistence very high (93.8–98.9%).
- **Evidence status:** CLAIMED, UNVERIFIED (specific metrics); peer-reviewed (Journal of Alternative Investments Vol 28, No 1, Summer 2025) — methodology externally validated but results paywalled
- **Source links:** [SSRN 5247471](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=5247471) · [Journal of Alt Investments](https://www.pm-research.com/content/iijaltinv/28/1/58)
- **Verification level:** Peer-reviewed (strong positive); methodology paywalled; no open code; no accessible performance metrics
- **Analyst note:** The peer-review is the key distinguishing feature among today's gold strategies. Consistent with Dujava's simpler cross-asset approach — convergent independent evidence. Key limitation: specific TAA rules, PCA features, and all performance metrics (Sharpe, CAGR, drawdown) are behind the paywall; cannot verify or implement without the paper. Regime 3 appeared in Dec 2008 and hasn't ended — limited OOS testing. Score: 46 (Experimental).

## Emerging Patterns

1. **Regime-conditional gold — convergent independent evidence:** Two unrelated research teams (Dujava/Quantpedia with a simple 2-signal momentum rule; Bhansali/Suvak et al. with PCA + k-means) independently converge on the same finding: gold's regime matters more than its absolute level. Both find that gold performs well when falling-rate / risk-off regimes dominate, and both were published in late 2024 – 2025. This convergence is meaningful.

2. **Cost modeling separates real from fake edges:** The crypto-ts-xs-momentum paper (Han et al.) is the highest-scoring crypto strategy precisely because it does what most practitioners skip — it models 15bps costs explicitly and still finds a surviving TS edge. The contrast with bitcoin-multitf-trend (same ballpark returns, zero cost modeling) is stark: one is a credible finding, the other is presumptively invalid.

3. **Vojtko/Quantpedia Bitcoin coverage saturation:** This run added the third Bitcoin strategy from the same research group (bitcoin-multitf-trend alongside bitcoin-max-min-trendrev and fx-mean-reversion-futures-monthly). All share: same dataset, no cost modeling, no OOS. The correlated evidence is noted; future runs should actively diversify away from this group.
