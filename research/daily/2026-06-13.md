- **Score:** 28 (Low Confidence) | latent 28 (capped to 28 — evidence auditability 2/10;
  paper disclaims performance claims; SSRN preprint only; no peer review; no open code;
  cost modeling absent; no OOS evidence)
- **Asset class:** Forex (pairs NOT SPECIFIED)
- **Timeframe:** Intraday (exact TF NOT REPORTED)
- **Core concept:** Bhatti (SSRN 6087107, January 2026) proposes conditioning intraday FX
  mean-reversion entries on a higher-timeframe momentum regime filter. Z-score of price
  deviation (volatility-normalized) signals entry when price has deviated sufficiently from
  its rolling mean. Mean-reversion trades are suppressed during momentum/trending regimes
  identified by the multi-TF filter. Volatility-adjusted position sizing. MQL5 prototype
  referenced but not confirmed publicly available.
- **Evidence status:** NO EVIDENCE — paper explicitly makes no performance claims
- **Source links:**
  - SSRN 6087107: https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=6087107 (HTTP 403)
- **Verification level:** None — conceptual framework paper
- **Analyst note:** The underlying microstructure mechanism (dealer inventory mean reversion)
  is legitimate and well-documented in the FX academic literature. The regime-conditioning
  design is sensible. However: (1) the edge is captured primarily at HFT timescales, not
  retail 1-5 min resolution; (2) no parameters are disclosed; (3) retail spread/slippage
  costs in intraday FX almost certainly erase any gross edge; (4) the paper itself declines
  to make any performance claim. This is a framework description, not a validated strategy.
  Score 28 correctly reflects near-absence of deployable evidence.
