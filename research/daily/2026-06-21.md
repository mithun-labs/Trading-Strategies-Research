## Strategies This Pass (ranked by confidence)

### 1. Dividend Reinvestment Market Timing (Market-Wide Predictable Price Pressure)

- **Score:** 57 (Experimental) | latent 57 (no cap: Evidence auditability 8 — AER peer-reviewed, documented methodology)
- **Asset class:** US Equity Index (S&P 500 / SPY / ES futures); international equity indices
- **Timeframe:** Daily (1-2 day hold; buy before or at open of high-dividend day, sell next close)
- **Core concept:** Aggregate dollar value of dividends paid to shareholders on any given day is
  publicly known weeks in advance from dividend announcements. On high-payment days, institutional
  investors receive cash and reinvest broadly into the equity market — creating predictable uninformed
  buying pressure that lifts aggregate returns. The strategy goes long the equity index scaled by
  quintile of daily aggregate payout yield, using 2× leverage on the highest-payout quintile and
  flat/zero on the lowest. A combined variant (DIV+FOMC) adds leverage on FOMC announcement days.
- **Evidence status:** AUDITABLE (AER peer-reviewed methodology; CRSP data) at the factor level;
  CLAIMED, UNVERIFIED for the trading strategy performance (no Sharpe/drawdown accessible)
- **Source links:**
  - AER paper: https://www.aeaweb.org/articles?id=10.1257/aer.20231725 (retrieved 2026-06-21)
  - NBER WP 30688: https://www.nber.org/papers/w30688 (retrieved 2026-06-21)
- **Verification level:** AER = top-5 economics journal; peer-reviewed; CRSP data required for replication (commercial)
- **Analyst note:** The signal is among the most cleanly identified market timing signals in recent
  finance research — dividend payment dates carry no new economic information, making the price
  response pure flow-driven, not fundamentals-driven. The key unknowns are (1) net-of-cost performance,
  which is borderline at ~3 bps gross premium vs ~2 bps round-trip cost for SPY, and (2)
  post-publication arbitrage. The AEA publication means this is now well-known to all large
  institutions that receive AER. If the premium has been arbitraged down even 50%, the strategy
  would be marginal for retail but possibly still viable for futures traders. ANALYSIS: estimated
  net Sharpe ~0.15-0.25 for a practical implementation, which is modest. NOT an implementation
  candidate (latent 57 < 60 AND key performance metrics inaccessible).

## Emerging Patterns

- **Flow-driven market timing is gaining AER-level academic legitimacy:** The Hartzmark/Solomon
  AER paper (September 2025) joins a small set of peer-reviewed papers documenting that predictable,
  non-information-based flows generate systematic market timing signals. This is distinct from
  traditional factor investing in that the signal is based on when cash arrives, not fundamental
  characteristics. The PreTOM momentum effect (`intramonth-momentum-pretom`, score 61) is a
  related flow-based phenomenon. Both suggest that institutional cash flows create predictable
  price pressure that can be traded.

- **HTTP 403 / paywall barrier remains severe:** 14 of 16 source attempts returned 403 or
  required institutional login. All SSRN PDFs inaccessible. Research quality continues to be
  constrained by this infrastructure limitation. The strategy researched today (`dividend-reinvestment-
  market-timing`) was possible only because the key numerical results appeared in secondary sources
  (IDEAS/RePEC abstract, web search snippets). Had the abstract itself been paywalled, a full
  strategy file would not have been possible.

- **Post-publication arbitrage risk is growing:** With the AER 2025 paper now public, the
  dividend payment day premium is known to all AER readers (i.e., all professional finance
  researchers and institutional quants). Unlike SSRN working papers (which have smaller audiences),
  AER papers reach the full institutional quant community within weeks of publication. The ~9-month
  window since September 2025 publication may have been sufficient for major institutions to trade
  against this signal. This is a generalizable concern for any strategy published in a top-5 journal:
  the very quality of evidence that lifts the confidence score also guarantees broad institutional
  awareness that may erode the edge.

## Red Flags Detected

- **No trading strategy Sharpe or drawdown from accessible sources:** The AER paper's key claim
  (top quintile 4× bottom quintile) is a factor-level finding. What this implies for a practical
  trading strategy — net Sharpe, maximum drawdown, tail behavior — is NOT REPORTED from accessible
  summaries. This is the most important gap.
- **Post-publication arbitrage:** The effect has been public since September 2025. Institutional
  front-running may have significantly reduced the premium.
- **Cost sensitivity:** At ~3.2 bps gross premium per SD vs ~2 bps round-trip for SPY, the
  margin is thin. Futures would be necessary for robust positive net performance.
- **Declining payout yields:** Corporate shift toward buybacks reduces the aggregate dividend
  cash flow magnitude, potentially weakening the effect in the post-2000 era.
