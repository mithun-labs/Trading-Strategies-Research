## Strategies This Pass (ranked by confidence)

### 1. Active Dual Momentum GTAA — Score 30 (Low Confidence)
- **Asset class:** Multi-asset ETFs (SHY, IEF, UUP, GLD, USO, SPY, EFA, QQQ, EEM)
- **Timeframe:** Weekly (Wednesday close rebalance)
- **Core concept:** Combines 10-week and 25-week cross-sectional momentum signals on
  9 multi-asset ETFs at 50/50 weight. Top-3 ETFs by each ROC measure are held if
  their absolute momentum is positive; otherwise cash. The strategy is a GTAA
  application of Antonacci's dual momentum framework. Blending two horizons aims to
  reduce lookback sensitivity.
- **Evidence status:** CLAIMED, UNVERIFIED — Quantpedia blog post by a junior analyst.
  No peer review, no independent replication, no open code, zero costs assumed.
- **Source links:**
  - https://quantpedia.com/active-dual-momentum-gtaa-strategy/ (primary)
  - https://quantpedia.com/quantpedia-in-may-2026/ (discovery)
- **Verification level:** None — blog only
- **Analyst note:** The mechanism is grounded in well-documented academic anomalies
  (Jegadeesh-Titman 1993, Moskowitz et al. 2012, Antonacci 2014), but this specific
  implementation has three critical problems: (1) zero costs on weekly rebalancing
  are presumptively invalid — estimated friction of 260–520 bps/year; (2) full
  in-sample parameter grid without OOS; (3) universe selection is an uncontrolled
  "hidden parameter" acknowledged by the author. Post-2015 live tracking of similar
  Dual Momentum GTAA strategies shows consistent decay to Sharpe 0.3–0.6 vs. the
  backtested ~0.9. Not an implementation candidate.

## Red Flags Detected

1. **Active Dual Momentum GTAA:** Zero costs on weekly-rebalanced multi-asset ETF
   strategy — explicit in the article, unquantified, and presumptively invalidating.
2. **Universe selection as hidden parameter:** The article itself flags this issue but
   provides no sensitivity analysis. Any addition or removal of an ETF could
   significantly change reported performance.
3. **Full in-sample parameter search:** ~3,888+ parameter combinations tested without
   OOS; optimal selection is then reported as the strategy.
4. **Benchmark gaming:** The equal-weight 9-ETF benchmark is an unusually weak
   comparison point (no risk-free return; heavily damaged by 2008–2009 crisis).

  (all blog-sourced implementations). Academic evidence base: Jegadeesh-Titman (1993),
