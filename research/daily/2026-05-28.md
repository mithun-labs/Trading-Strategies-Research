## Strategies This Pass (ranked by confidence)

### 1. Opening Range Breakout — Stocks In Play (ORB-SIP)
- **Score:** 78 — HIGH POTENTIAL
- **Asset class:** US Equities (intraday)
- **Timeframe:** 5-minute
- **Core concept:** Trade the 5-minute opening range breakout exclusively on "Stocks in Play"
  — equities with abnormally high volume driven by overnight fundamental news. First 5-min
  candle color sets the permitted trade direction; ATR-based stop; exit at end of day.
- **Evidence status:** CLAIMED, UNVERIFIED (academic paper: Zarattini, Barbon, Aziz, 2024,
  SSRN 4729284, SFI Research Paper 24-98) + independent replication on QuantConnect
- **Source links:**
  - [SSRN 4729284](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=4729284)
  - [QuantConnect Research Notebook](https://www.quantconnect.com/research/18444/)
- **Verification level:** Independent partial replication exists (QuantConnect)
- **Analyst note:** The volume/news filter is the critical differentiator. Without it, ORB
  has no clear edge. The independent QuantConnect replication (Sharpe 2.4) corroborates the
  paper's directional finding, though specific numbers differ. Maximum drawdown NOT REPORTED
  — a gap that should be filled before implementation research.

### 2. Catching Crypto Trends — Donchian Channel Ensemble (CCT-DCE)
- **Score:** 64 — WORTH RESEARCHING
- **Asset class:** Cryptocurrency (BTC + top-20 liquid altcoins)
- **Timeframe:** Daily
- **Core concept:** Ensemble of nine Donchian channel trend models (lookbacks: 5, 10, 20, 30,
  60, 90, 150, 250, 360 days) with volatility-based position sizing. Long-only, daily rebalancing.
  Tested on Bitcoin (CAGR 30%, Sharpe 1.58, max DD 19%) and altcoin rotation portfolio.
- **Evidence status:** CLAIMED, UNVERIFIED (academic paper: Zarattini, Pagani, Barbon, 2025,
  SSRN 5209907, SFI Research Paper 25-80)
- **Source links:**
  - [SSRN 5209907](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=5209907)
  - [abarbon.com](https://www.abarbon.com/papers/catching-crypto-trends)
- **Verification level:** No independent replication found
- **Analyst note:** The drawdown reduction (19% vs. Bitcoin's 80%+) is the headline result.
  Long-only bias inflates CAGR in crypto's bull-biased environment. Exit rules and volatility
  formula not fully retrieved; independent verification needed. No walk-forward test confirmed.

## Emerging Patterns

1. **Volume/news-filtered breakouts outperform generic breakouts.** The ORB-SIP evidence
   strongly suggests that the presence of a fundamental driver (abnormal volume) is what
   differentiates a breakout with edge from random noise. The London Breakout, lacking this
   filter, shows no reliable edge. This pattern suggests that screening for "catalysts" before
   applying any breakout strategy is a critical success factor.

2. **Trend-following in crypto outperforms buy-and-hold in risk-adjusted terms.** Multiple
   sources (Zarattini 2025, AdaptiveTrend) suggest that systematic trend models reduce
   drawdown dramatically vs. passive crypto exposure, even if absolute CAGR is lower.

3. **Same research group (Zarattini/Barbon) active across equities and crypto.** Their
   methodology is reproducible and they focus on practical implementation including transaction
   costs. Their papers are worth tracking systematically.
