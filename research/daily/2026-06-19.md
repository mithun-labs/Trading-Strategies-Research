## Strategies This Pass (ranked by confidence)

### 1. ATH Breakout Stock Trend Following — Zarattini, Pagani, Wilcox (SSRN 5084316, January 2025)

- **Score:** 51 (Experimental) | latent 51 (evidence auditability 4/10: SSRN working paper, not peer-reviewed, no open code, methodology described clearly; cap 59 does not bind since latent 51 < 59)
- **Asset class:** US Equities (NYSE + AMEX + NASDAQ, survivorship-bias-free 1950–2024)
- **Timeframe:** Daily; positions held multi-week to multi-month
- **Core concept:** Buy any US stock that closes at a new all-time adjusted-close high; exit when price drops below a trailing ATR stop (stop only moves up, never down). Equal-weighted portfolio of all concurrent qualifying stocks; daily rebalancing. Extends Wilcox/Crittenden (2005) white paper to 74-year, 66,000+ trade database. Key finding: <7% of trades generate all cumulative profits — consistent with Bessembinder (2018) finding that most individual stocks underperform T-bills. A Turnover Control algorithm is introduced to mitigate "extensive daily turnover" and restore post-cost viability.
- **Evidence status:** CLAIMED, UNVERIFIED — SSRN working paper; performance numbers from author backtest; net-of-cost metrics not disclosed; TC algorithm proprietary
- **Source links:**
  - SSRN 5084316: https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=5084316
  - Concretum Group: https://concretumgroup.com/does-trend-following-still-work-on-stocks/
  - HarbourFront Quant: https://harbourfrontquant.substack.com/p/does-trend-following-still-work-on
  - Quantpedia: https://quantpedia.com/strategies/trend-following-effect-in-stocks/
- **Verification level:** SSRN working paper (Jan 2025); extends a widely-cited 2005 white paper (Wilcox/Crittenden); no independent academic peer review
- **Analyst note:** The mechanism (power-law stock return distribution → ATH breakout captures outlier winners) is economically sound and well-documented (Bessembinder 2018). The 74-year survivorship-bias-free dataset with genuine 20-year OOS is a methodological strength. However, the strategy's viability hinge entirely on the proprietary Turnover Control algorithm — the gross CAGR 15.19% and Max DD 31.75% are not independently useful without a Sharpe ratio or net-of-cost figures. The `industry-trend-century` paper (same lead author, score 64) applies the same trend philosophy to industry ETFs at far lower turnover and achieves better disclosed metrics. The individual-stock ATH breakout version is therefore inferior to the industry version on a cost-and-transparency-adjusted basis. NOT an implementation candidate.

## Emerging Patterns

- **Zarattini suite breadth:** This run's strategy is the fourth Zarattini paper in this database (`industry-trend-century`, `intraday-momentum-spy` via earlier SSRN, `fast-alphas-intraday-overlay`, and now `ath-breakout-stock-trend-following`). Consistent pattern: strong gross metrics, high turnover, cost concerns addressed by proprietary methodology. The industry-trends paper (with CMT Award endorsement and 98-year dataset) remains the strongest in this author's suite.
- **Gross vs. net opacity:** A recurring theme across 2025-2026 papers is presenting gross backtests prominently while leaving net-of-cost results absent or addressed by methods that cannot be replicated (proprietary algorithms, undisclosed parameters). This is not unique to Zarattini — it appears in multiple reviewed papers across this database.
- **Power-law / outlier return concentration:** The <7% of trades driving all profits is the same mechanism underlying `intramonth-momentum-pretom` (momentum concentrated in PreTOM window), `cmm-daily-return-weighted-momentum` (EA-day return concentration), and now ATH breakout. These are all variations on the theme that stock return distributions are highly skewed and most money-making is concentrated in a small number of events.
