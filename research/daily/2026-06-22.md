## Strategies This Pass (ranked by confidence)

### 1. REIT Factor Momentum

- **Score:** 33 (Low Confidence) | latent 33 (not capped: cap = 59, raw score 33 < cap; held back by missing parameters, unconfirmed costs, binary 4-factor universe)
- **Asset class:** US REITs (Real Estate Investment Trusts)
- **Timeframe:** Monthly
- **Core concept:** Apply the factor momentum principle (well-documented in equity markets by Ehsani/Linnainmaa 2021) to the US REIT market. Rank four REIT factor portfolios (value, size, momentum, profitability) by their recent performance; go long the top-2 factors and short the bottom-2, rebalancing monthly. The paper's key diagnostic: traditional static REIT factors show "unstable and atypical behavior" vs equity markets, but rotating dynamically into current factor winners captures significant alpha.
- **Evidence status:** CLAIMED, UNVERIFIED — all performance metrics from secondary source (QuantSeeker recap) summarizing an SSRN preprint. Full paper behind 403 barrier.
- **Source links:**
  - SSRN 5631072: https://ssrn.com/abstract=5631072 (retrieved 2026-06-22, HTTP 403)
  - QuantSeeker recap: https://www.quantseeker.com/p/weekly-research-recap-97f (retrieved 2026-06-22)
- **Verification level:** SSRN preprint only; not peer-reviewed; primary PDF inaccessible; performance data from secondary summary only
- **Analyst note:** The mechanism (factor momentum) is economically grounded, but the 4-factor universe is extremely small — going long 2 of 4 and short 2 of 4 is a binary bet, not genuine cross-sectional ranking. The pre-cost Sharpe of 1.35 is plausible but REIT short-selling costs could consume most of the gross 5.7% annual return. Critical parameters (formation period, backtest dates, max drawdown) NOT REPORTED. NOT an implementation candidate (latent 33 < 60).

## Emerging Patterns

- **Factor momentum as a cross-asset signal:** Factor momentum now appears in equities (Ehsani/Linnainmaa 2021), CTA/managed futures (general factor rotation), FX (prior runs), and now explicitly in REITs (Dobrynskaya et al. 2025). The pattern is consistent: static factor exposure is often unstable, but rotating dynamically into currently-winning factors captures alpha. The mechanism is the same (factor return autocorrelation driven by slow capital flows), but each market has unique cost structures that determine net viability.

- **HTTP 403 barrier severity remains high:** Both SSRN (primary paper) and Alpha Architect (secondary review article) returned 403. 6 of 14 fetches were blocked. This systematically prevents access to key methodological details — formation period, backtest dates, max drawdown — that would be decisive for scoring. The research quality floor is structurally limited by this barrier.

- **Small-universe factor rotation papers:** Both `reit-factor-momentum` (4 factors, long 2/short 2) and `dynamic-factor-allocation-regime-shu-mulvey` (6 factors, regime-conditioned weights) involve rotating within a small set of factors. The statistical power concern is real in both cases: with 4–6 factors, identifying persistent rotation patterns is statistically tenuous, and the likelihood of data-mining is higher than with large cross-sections.

## Red Flags Detected

- **REIT factor momentum: formation period NOT REPORTED** — the most critical unknown for replication. Without knowing whether the formation period is 1, 3, 6, or 12 months, independent verification is impossible.
- **Short-selling costs unaddressed:** The entire short leg of the REIT factor momentum strategy may be economically infeasible at the implied gross return (5.7% annual) once realistic borrow costs (5–15% annualized for illiquid REITs) are applied.
- **Binary universe problem:** Long-2/short-2 from 4 factors is a degenerate case of factor momentum that has very low power to distinguish genuine signal from random factor ordering.
