## Strategies This Pass (ranked by confidence)

### 1. SPXW Short-Dated Put-Writing with Kelly/VIX Dynamic Sizing (Wysocki 2025)

- **Score:** 42 (Experimental)
- **Asset class:** US Equity Index Options (SPXW / SPX weekly and daily options)
- **Timeframe:** Intraday/daily (0–1 DTE optimal)
- **Core concept:** Sell SPXW put options approximately 2% out-of-the-money with 0–5 days to
  expiry, using dynamic position sizing: Kelly criterion, VIX-based vol-scaling, or a hybrid
  combining both. The paper's contribution is evaluating these sizing methods for ultra-short-dated
  index put writing, finding that the Garman-Klass 63-day volatility estimator with ~2% OTM
  1 DTE puts produces the best annualized return in the optimal parameter configuration.
- **Evidence status:** CLAIMED, UNVERIFIED — arXiv preprint (Wysocki, August 2025); no peer
  review; single author; all accessible mirrors HTTP 403; abstract and key findings extracted
  only from web search snippets
- **Source links:**
  - https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.16598 (arXiv preprint, August 2025)
- **Verification level:** arXiv preprint, no peer review. Evidence auditability = 2/10.
  Capped at 59; latent 42 is below cap.
- **Analyst note:** The VRP mechanism is one of the best-established risk premia in finance.
  The paper's bid-ask cost modeling is a genuine strength. The primary weakness is the
  undisclosed parameter selection from a "broad design space" with no OOS validation —
  standard in-sample selection bias. The 20-25% CAGR for the "optimal" configuration almost
  certainly reflects the best in-sample parameters, not forward-looking expectations. Two
  pending candidates added to queue for future runs.

## Red Flags Detected

1. **SPXW put-writing: no crash-period validation.** The Wysocki paper's backtest likely
   covers 2022–2025. This misses COVID March 2020, Volmageddon Feb 2018, and the August 2015
   China flash crash — all events that would materially test a short put strategy. Without
   these, the max drawdown figure cannot be estimated from published metrics.

2. **In-sample parameter selection from broad grid.** The Wysocki paper evaluates moneyness
   levels, volatility estimators, and memory horizons without a described OOS test. Selecting
   "Garman-Klass, 63-day, 2% OTM, 0-1 DTE" as the best combination is the output of an
   in-sample search, not a robust finding.

3. **Win-rate characteristic of short-vol strategies.** Short put strategies typically have
   high win rates (80–90%+) but fat left tails. The win rate is NOT REPORTED for this paper.
   Without the full loss distribution, it is impossible to evaluate whether the strategy
   satisfies the VRP conditions or is an uncompensated tail risk exposure.
