## Strategies This Pass (ranked by confidence)

### 1. Commodity Dual Momentum — Sector ETF

**Score**: 42 (Experimental) | **Asset class**: Commodities | **Timeframe**: Monthly
**Core concept**: Antonacci dual momentum applied to 4 commodity sector ETFs (DBA agriculture,
DBB base metals, DBE energy, DBP precious metals). Monthly end-of-month rebalancing: rank all
4 by 3-month ROC, invest equally in top 3 only if each is above its 3-month SMA; otherwise
hold cash for that slot.
**Evidence status**: CLAIMED, UNVERIFIED — Quantpedia blog post by Sona Beluska (June 15, 2026)
**Source links**: Quantpedia blog (June 2026)
**Verification level**: Blog post only; no academic peer review; no independent replication
**Analyst note**: The dual momentum mechanism substantially reduces max drawdown versus pure
relative momentum (-30% vs -64%) at the cost of lower absolute returns (7.54% vs 11.51%). The
main weaknesses are the trivially small 4-ETF universe (ranking 4 items offers minimal
discrimination), no OOS testing, and costs not modeled. Nonetheless, the strategy is honest
about its drawdown and the mechanism is well-grounded in Antonacci's documented framework.
Similar to `commodity-intramarket-correlation-momentum` (same universe, different mechanism).

### 2. ESG State-Dependent Momentum

**Score**: 31 (Low Confidence) | **Asset class**: US Equities + Crypto | **Timeframe**: Biweekly
**Core concept**: Latent 2-state ESG Markov chain (pro/anti-ESG) conditions long-short
momentum on PCA-reduced Russell 3000 portfolios. Key counterintuitive finding: ESG losers
(Sharpe 0.973) outperform ESG winners (Sharpe 0.657) in Russell 3000 (2017-2025). But the
W-L momentum spread itself has Sharpe = -0.316 (negative), meaning classic momentum fails and
the paper is essentially arguing that a regime flip rescues a failing signal.
**Evidence status**: CLAIMED, UNVERIFIED — arXiv preprint (May 2025), no peer review
**Source links**: arXiv 2505.24250
**Verification level**: Preprint; Bloomberg data required; PCA-based implementation non-replicable with public data
**Analyst note**: Interesting counterintuitive finding but undermined by: (1) negative W-L
Sharpe in primary market (Russell 3000: -0.316), (2) no costs modeled, (3) DJ30 spread has
positive Sharpe (0.367) but negative -43.1% cumulative return over 4 years (arithmetic-
geometric divergence = extreme volatility), (4) short biased periods for DJ30/Crypto (start
Nov 2020 = COVID trough), (5) PCA implementation not replicable as individual-stock long-short.
The energy-sector rebound explanation (ESG losers = energy stocks outperforming 2021-2022)
is more parsimonious than the ESG regime mechanism.

## Emerging Patterns

1. **Antonacci dual momentum saturation**: Three entries in the database now apply the same
   dual momentum framework to different asset universes (multi-asset ETFs, gold+BTC, commodity
   sector ETFs). All score 30–49. The framework is simple and widely applicable, but its
   evidence base remains blog-level for specific implementations. The framework itself has
   peer-reviewed support (Antonacci 2014 book and associated research), but each specific
   application needs independent validation.

2. **4-ETF commodity universe limitation**: Both commodity ETF strategies in the database
   (this one and `commodity-intramarket-correlation-momentum`) use the same 4 ETFs (DBA, DBB,
   DBE, DBP). Ranking or switching signals within such a small universe offer limited
   information. Futures-based commodity strategies with broader universes (e.g.,
   `commodity-futures-curve-dynamics-ns`, `commodity-basis-reversal-futures`,
   `tsmom-china-commodity-futures`) offer substantially stronger evidence.

3. **Quantpedia blog-only strategies**: Several strategies in the database (score 30–42) come
   from Quantpedia blog posts without underlying peer-reviewed papers. These consistently score
   low due to lack of OOS testing and absent cost modeling.
